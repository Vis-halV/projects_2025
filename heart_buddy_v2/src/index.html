High Cholesterol

High cholesterol levels can lead to the buildup of plaque in the arteries, narrowing them and restricting blood flow to the heart, which increases the risk of heart attacks.

Prevention Tips

Healthy Diet

Maintain a heart-healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Limit saturated fats, trans fats, and excessive sodium to reduce cholesterol and blood pressure.

Regular Exercise

Engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity weekly. Regular exercise strengthens the heart, improves circulation, and helps maintain a healthy weight.

Stress Management

Practice stress-reduction techniques like meditation, deep breathing, or yoga. Chronic stress can contribute to heart disease by raising blood pressure and inflammation.

Regular Check-ups

Schedule regular health screenings to monitor blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar levels. Early detection and management of risk factors can prevent heart disease.
